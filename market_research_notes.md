## Market Research: Second-Hand Fashion in Thailand (2024-2025)

### 1. Market Size & Growth
*   **Massive Potential:** The Thailand Second-hand Apparel (Thrift) Market is projected to grow from **USD 5.2 billion in 2025 to USD 17.9 billion by 2031**, with a **CAGR of 22.8%**.
*   **Key Drivers:** Affordability, Sustainability, and the rise of digital resale platforms.

### 2. Gen Z: The "Early Force" & "Trend Setters"
*   **Economic Power:** Gen Z (aged 13-28) in Thailand holds an economic value exceeding **2.6 trillion baht**.
*   **Behavior:**
    *   **Digital First:** 71% of Gen Z and Millennials prefer to buy secondhand apparel online.
    *   **Self-Expression:** They spend primarily on **fashion, beauty, and entertainment**. Purchases are driven by "emotional significance" and "self-identity," not just price.
    *   **Research-Oriented:** University students compare prices (78%) and research independently (61%).
    *   **Sustainability:** Rising environmental concerns are driving demand for "circular fashion."

### 3. The Shift in Perception
*   **From "Necessity" to "Lifestyle":** Second-hand is no longer just for the poor. It is "trendy," "unique," and a "smart choice."
*   **Demand for Uniqueness:** Consumers want "unique, vintage, or branded items" that fast fashion cannot offer.
*   **Trust Gap:** While interest is high, "trust" remains a key barrier. Brands that can bridge the trust gap (transparency, authentication) will win.

### Implications for ShopDer
*   **Timing is Perfect:** The market is at an inflection point (beginning of a 22.8% CAGR growth curve).
*   **Target Audience:** Gen Z is the undeniable beachhead market. They have the money (2.6T baht), the motivation (sustainability + uniqueness), and the behavior (online first).
*   **Problem to Solve:** Connecting the "Digital First" preference with the "Trust/Discovery" gap in current platforms.
